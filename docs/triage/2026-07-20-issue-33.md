## Assessment

**Substantive and in-scope** (the page's own reference is 2026/0138(COD) = `tracker.yaml` `file_id`).

**But the issue's own reading of the rule numbers is inverted, and the plan corrects it.** Under the
**10th-term (2024–2029) Rules of Procedure** — the ones in force — the numbering shifted from the
9th term:

| Rule (10th term, in force) | Title |
|---|---|
| **58** | Budgetary assessment on proposals for legally binding acts with budgetary implications ([RoP Rule 58](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/doceo/document/RULES-10-2025-07-07-RULE-058_EN.html)) |
| **59** | **Joint committee procedure** (ex-Rule 58 in the 9th term) ([RoP Rule 59](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/doceo/document/RULES-10-2025-07-07-RULE-059_EN.html)) |

So the issue's claim that "Rule 58 = joint committee" is 9th-term numbering. The **newly added**
basis is **Rule 59 — the joint committee procedure**; Rule 58 (budgetary assessment) is the routine
baseline that pairs with the "Committee for budgetary assessment" field already on the page.

**Control check (decisive).** The sibling file **2026/0139 (COD)** (Chips Act 2.0 — `keep_apart`,
tracked elsewhere) was fetched the same day for comparison: it reads plain "**Committee responsible**"
and its legal basis lists **"Rules of Procedure EP 58" only**. Same package, same day, same OEIL
template — which confirms (a) Rule 58 is the standard baseline on both, and (b) the **Rule 59 /
joint-committee flag is specific to CADA**, not an OEIL-wide template change. This also resolves the
issue's "manual check needed": the wording is a genuine new OEIL edit, not a long-standing entry the
repo failed to capture.

**Why it matters.** Rule 59 applies where a matter falls within the competence of two or three
committees **without any one of them prevailing**: the Conference of Presidents may decide on joint
committee meetings with a **joint vote**, each committee appoints **one rapporteur**, chairing
alternates, and the committees count as a **single committee** for quorum/majority purposes. That
displaces this repo's standing "ITRE is the prospective lead" framing — there will be **two or three
co-lead committees**, not one lead plus opinion-givers.

**What is *not* established.** OEIL does **not** yet name the committees, and the referral itself is
still "pending final decision". ITRE remains the strongest candidate (Greens/EFA shadow Geese sits
there); IMCO is a plausible partner given the internal-market legal basis (Art 114 TFEU) and
Ballarín Cereza's IMCO coordinator role — but **that pairing is inference, not sourced fact**, and is
recorded only as a hedged watch item. Consistent with pre-referral status, `T1-13` (ITRE committee
documents) returned zero in-scope documents the same day.

No feature of the proposal is affected — this is procedure only, so no `extracts/` or
`docs/what-changed.md` check is needed.

# Summary — what this file does

The **Cloud and AI Development Act (CADA)** — COM(2026) 502 final, file 2026/0138 (COD) — is the
Commission's proposal (3 June 2026) to reduce Europe's dependence on non-EU cloud and AI infrastructure.
It is the legislative core of the **European Technological Sovereignty Package**, presented together
with Chips Act 2.0 (file 2026/0139, tracked elsewhere), the EU Open Source Strategy and a Strategic
Roadmap for Digitalisation and AI in Energy. The Commission's framing: the EU depends on non-EU
providers for over 80% of its digital products, services and infrastructure (Draghi report), and three
non-European hyperscalers hold over 70% of the EU cloud market.

In plain language, the proposal (48 articles, five Titles — a standalone regulation that amends
nothing) does five things:

1. **Cloud and AI Leadership Initiatives** (Arts 3–9) — research/innovation support, Experience and
   Acceleration Centres for AI, national cloud-and-AI strategies, "frontier AI priority projects".
2. **Data-centre capacity** (Arts 10–15) — Member States must designate **acceleration zones** with
   faster permitting and single information points; the Commission designates **strategic projects**
   and monitors the capacity gap. Goal: triple EU data-centre capacity in 5–7 years (~€200bn, mostly
   private investment) — see [provisions/data-centre-acceleration-zones.md](provisions/data-centre-acceleration-zones.md).
3. **EU Cloud Sovereignty Framework** (Arts 16–28) — four **Union assurance levels** for cloud
   services (criteria in Annex II: control over the service and supply chain, data handling,
   infrastructure location, cybersecurity); self-assessment for level 1, third-party audits for
   levels 2–4, national competent authorities, penalties — see
   [provisions/sovereignty-framework.md](provisions/sovereignty-framework.md).
4. **Public-sector use and procurement** (Arts 29–40) — risk assessments determine which assurance
   level a public body must procure; bodies whose activities have public-order relevance (incl. NIS2
   sectors) may only use levels 2–4; a **EuroCloud Federation** for the public sector; a Commission-run
   common procurement framework — see [provisions/public-sector-use.md](provisions/public-sector-use.md).
5. **Open source** (Arts 41–44) — public bodies must prefer open-source over proprietary solutions,
   share and reuse publicly-developed software, with a Commission Open Source Solutions Catalogue and
   a network of national OSPOs — see [provisions/open-source.md](provisions/open-source.md).

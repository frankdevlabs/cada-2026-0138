# Institutional positions — Commission vs Council vs Parliament

> Per-provision detail lives in [`data/positions.csv`](../data/positions.csv) and
> [`provisions/`](provisions/); this page is the prose overview. The one-screen current state is in
> [`STATUS.md`](../STATUS.md).

| Institution | Stage | Headline position |
|---|---|---|
| **Commission** | proposer | Standalone sovereignty regulation: four assurance levels, capacity tripling, public-sector procurement obligations, open-source-first |
| **Council** | co-legislator | None yet — proposal circulated 4 June 2026 (ST 10104/26, Telecom WP); first policy debate at the Telecom Council of 9 June 2026 |
| **Parliament** | co-legislator | None yet — no committee referral, no rapporteur (ITRE expected) |

The file is at the very start of the ordinary legislative procedure: the only institutional text is the
Commission proposal itself. The first formal position signals to watch (see
[`STATUS.md` milestones](../STATUS.md#next-milestones-to-watch)):

- **Council** — the first Presidency compromise text (a new ST number on this file's register stream)
  will mark where delegations start pulling; expected under the Irish Presidency (H2 2026). Until WK
  delegation-comment streams appear, member-state signals come from non-papers — see
  [`member-state-positions.md`](member-state-positions.md).
- **Parliament** — committee referral and rapporteur appointment; the pre-proposal
  [EPRS briefing](../sources/README.md) (Dec 2025) sketches the option space Parliament was given.
- **EESC** — opinion in preparation
  ([opinion page](https://www.eesc.europa.eu/en/our-work/opinions-information-reports/opinions/cloud-and-ai-development-act)).

Early friction lines between the institutions-to-be are catalogued in
[`fault-lines.md`](fault-lines.md).

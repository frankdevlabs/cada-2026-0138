# Open source: preference, reuse, catalogue, OSPO network (Title IV ch VI, Arts 41–44)

> **Baseline: Commission proposal COM(2026) 502.** Sourced from the explanatory memorandum and press
> material — **pending verification against the operative text**. No co-legislator position yet.

## What the proposal does

- **Art 41** — Union entities and public-sector bodies must **encourage and facilitate the use of
  open-source solutions over proprietary ones** (the legislative anchor of the package's
  "open source first" principle).
- **Art 42** — requirements on **sharing and reuse** of software developed by or for Union entities and
  public-sector bodies.
- **Art 43** — Commission-maintained **Open Source Solutions Catalogue**.
- **Art 44** — a network of Member-State **Open Source Programme Offices (OSPOs)**.

## Context and friction

The non-legislative **EU Open Source Strategy** (same package) carries the scaling agenda — open-source
alternatives in cloud, AI, internet technologies, cybersecurity and semiconductors — and a reported
~€2bn investment envelope incl. an Open Source Maintenance Instrument. **Caveat:** the €2bn figure
circulates via secondary sources and is unconfirmed in official texts. MEP Groothuis criticised the lack
of investment in European open-source AI models — see [`fault-lines.md`](../fault-lines.md) and
[`stakeholders.md`](../stakeholders.md).

# Public-sector use, risk assessments and procurement (Title IV ch II–IV, Arts 29–40)

> **Baseline: Commission proposal COM(2026) 502.** Operative text:

## What the proposal does

- **Art 29** — Member States and Union entities conduct **risk assessments** to determine the required
  assurance level (2–4) for different public-sector activities.
- **Art 30** — contracting authorities procuring cloud services must procure **at minimum level 1**;
  where the risk assessment finds **public-order relevance**, only levels **2, 3 or 4** may be procured
  and used. Reported sector mapping: NIS2 sectors (digital infrastructure, energy, research, health)
  and national security, defence, border management, justice, law enforcement.
- **Art 31** — **private** NIS2-sector entities may conduct comparable impact assessments; the
  Commission can extend this via implementing acts (sleeper scope issue — see
  [`fault-lines.md`](../fault-lines.md)).
- **Art 32** — **Union added value criteria** in public procurement of cloud and AI within scope.
- **Art 33** — innovation-procurement monitoring + lean reporting, linked to national cloud/AI strategies.
- **Arts 34–36** — **EuroCloud Federation**: a European public-sector cloud federation, conditions for
  sharing data-centre/cloud services among members, Commission cost recovery.
- **Arts 37–40** — Commission-run **common procurement framework** (for Union entities, Member-State
  contracting authorities and selected partner organisations), incl. Financial Regulation derogations,
  governance, applicable procurement rules and fee-based cost compensation.

## Why it's contested

The level-1 floor for ordinary tasks and the room for national "à la carte" application are criticised
as too soft (no hard obligation to pick European providers — MEP van Sparrentak), while the 2–4
restriction for public-order-relevant activities is the de-facto market-shifting device aimed at
sovereignty concerns over non-EU hyperscalers.

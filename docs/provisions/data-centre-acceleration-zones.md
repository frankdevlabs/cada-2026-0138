# Data-centre capacity: acceleration zones and strategic projects (Title III, Arts 10–15)

> **Baseline: Commission proposal COM(2026) 502.** Sourced from the explanatory memorandum and press
> material — **pending verification against the operative text**. No co-legislator position yet.

## What the proposal does

- **Art 10** — Member States must designate **data centre acceleration zones** where deploying capacity.
- **Art 11** — conditions applicable within acceleration zones.
- **Art 12** — **single information points** for data-centre operators in the zones (new, or upgraded/
  integrated existing ones).
- **Art 13** — facilitated administrative and **permit-granting** processes for projects in the zones.
- **Art 14** — expression-of-interest mechanism and Commission designation of **data centre strategic
  projects**.
- **Art 15** — Commission monitoring of Union compute capacity, demand and the **capacity gap**.

## Ambition and friction

Goal: **triple EU data-centre capacity within 5–7 years** (Union has the capacity it needs by 2035),
requiring ~€200bn of mostly private investment. Friction points: financing, permitting backlogs and grid
congestion (DDA), and the interaction with the announced **Data Centre Energy Efficiency Package**
(EU rating-scheme delegated act; needs-analysis for minimum energy-performance standards) under the
package's energy roadmap — see [`fault-lines.md`](../fault-lines.md).

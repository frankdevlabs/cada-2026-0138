# EU Cloud Sovereignty Framework — Union assurance levels (Title IV ch I, Arts 16–28 + Annex II)

> **Baseline: Commission proposal COM(2026) 502.** Operative text:
> [extracts — Title IV Ch I, Arts 16–28](../../extracts/commission/COM-2026-502_sovereignty-framework-art16-28.md).
> No co-legislator has touched this provision yet.

## What the proposal does

- **Art 16 + Annex II** — a Union cloud computing sovereignty framework of **four assurance levels**,
  with requirements per level based on: control over the service, control over the supply chain, data
  handling, infrastructure location, and cybersecurity.
- **Art 17** — recognition mechanism: providers apply to the national competent authority of
  establishment for recognition at level 1–4.
- **Art 18** — a third **country** may be recognised as providing sufficient assurances, making services
  controlled from it eligible up to **level 3**.
- **Art 19** — level 1 = conformity **self-assessment**.
- **Arts 20–21** — levels 2–4 = **third-party audit** by auditing organisations; reported audit lenses
  include whether all personnel are EU-based, whether provider-generated data may be used for AI
  training, software supply-chain security measures, and a European cybersecurity certificate
  ("substantial" or higher). **Level 3 requires EU ownership and control** plus additional criteria
  (e.g. staff nationality).
- **Art 22** — Commission-maintained central repository of recognised services; **Art 23** transparency
  obligations on material changes; **Art 24** penalties and compensation.
- **Arts 25–28** — national competent authorities, their powers, mutual assistance, cross-border
  enforcement.

## Level semantics (reported)

- **Level 1** — lowest; usable for public tasks without public-order relevance (event management,
  tourism marketing, general administration).
- **Levels 2–4** — independent third-party audit required; level scales with sensitivity. Public bodies
  in NIS2 sectors (digital infrastructure, energy, research, health) or in national security, defence,
  border management, justice and law enforcement may only use levels 2–4 (see
  [public-sector-use](public-sector-use.md)).

## Why it's contested

The Commission states the **US CLOUD Act** is an obstacle for US providers to reach the highest levels,
and that the EU–US Data Privacy Framework does not remove sovereignty concerns ("operational autonomy").
Expect pressure on the Annex II criteria from Member States with established hyperscaler relationships —
see [`fault-lines.md`](../fault-lines.md).
